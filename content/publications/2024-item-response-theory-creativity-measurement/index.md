# Abstract

Item-response theory (IRT) represents a major advance in measurement theory, yet it is still often introduced narrowly and remains underused in creativity research. This book outlines the limitations of classical test theory in common creativity-measurement settings, introduces core IRT ideas through a broad and practical lens, and reviews applications relevant to creativity assessment. It also discusses strategic choices in modeling and identifies directions for future methodological development.
