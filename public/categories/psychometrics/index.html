Psychometrics

Solving the Paradox Between the Generalized Partial Credit Model and PISA’s Rating Instructions: A Generalized IRTree Approach to Within-Rating Multidimensionality

In this paper, published in Thinking Skills and Creativity, we show that PISA creative-thinking rating instructions conflict with the generalized partial credit model and propose a generalized IRTree approach to model within-rating multidimensionality.

Predictive Validity of the Algebra Concept Inventory (ACI): The Importance of Algebraic Conceptual Understanding to College Math and STEM Outcomes

In this paper, published in AERA Open, we show that scores on the Algebra Concept Inventory predict college math performance and STEM outcomes, highlighting the importance of algebraic conceptual understanding.

One Score, Two Components: Disentangling Appropriateness and Originality in PISA Creative Thinking Judgments Using Generalized Item Response Tree Models

In this paper, published in Journal of Creative Behavior, we disentangle appropriateness and originality in PISA creative-thinking judgments by modeling them as distinct latent components within a generalized item response tree framework.

Bayesian Estimation of Generalized Log-Linear Poisson Item Response Models for Fluency Scores Using brms and Stan

In this paper, published in Journal of Intelligence, we show how generalized log-linear Poisson item-response models for fluency scores (including the 2-Parameter Poisson Counts Model) can be estimated and interpreted in a Bayesian multilevel framework using brms and Stan.

Modeling Sequential Dependencies in Progressive Matrices: An Auto-Regressive Item Response Theory (AR-IRT) Approach

In this paper, published in Journal of Intelligence, we extend binary item-response models to include auto-regressive sequential dependencies, and show that AR-IRT can improve fit and yield more conservative parameter estimates in progressive matrices data.

Personality Traits Leading Respondents to Refuse to Answer a Forced-Choice Personality Item: An Item Response Tree (IRTree) Model

In this paper, published in Psych, we investigate which personality traits predict refusal to choose in forced-choice personality items, using an IRTree model to jointly model skipping behavior and reported reasons for refusal.

Unscrambling Creativity Measurement: An Invitation to Better Formalize the Domain Generality-Specificity of Creativity with Psychometric Modeling

In this paper, published in Learning and Individual Differences, we revisit how creativity is conceptualized and measured, and invite researchers to better formalize the domain generality-specificity question using explicit psychometric models.

Creativity with 6 Degrees of Freedom: Feasibility Study of Visual Creativity Assessment in Virtual Reality

In this paper, published in Creativity Research Journal, we evaluate the feasibility and psychometric properties of a visual creativity assessment implemented in immersive virtual reality, including evidence on structural validity, reliability, and criterion validity.

Can We “Read” Art…At All? A Psychometric Perspective on the Possibility of Measuring Artwork Attributes

In this paper, published in Physics of Life Reviews, we discuss from a psychometric perspective whether and how artwork attributes can be meaningfully measured, and what this implies for claims about “reading” art.

Exploring the Associations Between Personality and Response Speed Trajectories in Low-Stakes Intelligence Tests

In this paper, published in Personality and Individual Differences, using log-normal Item-Response Theory models for response time data, we study how personality traits predict response speed trajectories in low-stakes reasoning tasks. We found that some traits are associated with increasing in speed vs. decreasing in speed, controlling for item effects.

jrt – Item Response Theory Modeling and Scoring for Judgment Data

A package for the Psychometric analysis and scoring of judgment data using polytomous Item-Response Theory (IRT) models.

Accounting for Variable Task Discrimination in Divergent Thinking Fluency Measurement – An Example of the Benefits of a 2-Parameter Poisson Counts Model and its Bifactor Extension Over the Rasch Poisson Counts Model

In this paper, published in Journal of Creative Behavior, we introduce new psychometric models for count/fluency tasks (tasks in which individuals have to provide many instances). Notably, we extent the Rasch Poisson Counts Model (RPCM) to account for variable discrimination (2-Parameter Poisson Counts Model) and to account for local dependencies/nuisance factors (Bifactor 2-Parameter Poisson Counts Model).

A Mokken Scale Analysis of the Last Series of the Standard Progressive Matrices (SPM-LS)

In this paper, published in Journal of Intelligence, I reanalyze a dataset for a special issue of Journal of Intelligence. The analysis uses the framework of Mokken Scale Analysis (MSA), which is a non-parametric approach to relations between item responses and attributes (i.e. Item-Response Theory).

Using Visual Aesthetic Sensitivity Measures in Museum Studies

In this paper, published in Frontiers in Psychology, we discuss how visual aesthetic sensitivity measures can be used in museum research, with a focus on validity considerations and measurement choices.

Confirmation of a Two-Factor Solution to the Questionnaire of Cognitive and Affective Empathy in Schizophrenia

In this paper, published in Frontiers in Psychiatry, we confirm a two-factor structure of cognitive and affective empathy in schizophrenia.

Same Test, Better Scores – Boosting the Reliability of Short Online Intelligence Recruitment Tests with Nested Logit Item Response Theory Models

In this paper, published in Journal of Intelligence, we propose to generalize the use of Nested Logit Models and implement its use for online personnel selection procedures that use reasoning tests with multiple choices (e.g., matrix-type tests). We find that using such models results in gains of reliability, especially at low ability levels.

Judge Response Theory? A Call to Upgrade Our Psychometrical Account of Creativity Judgments

In this paper, published in Psychology of Aesthetics, Creativity, and the Arts, we argue for judge response theory as an improved psychometric account of creativity ratings.

Career Decision Ambiguity Tolerance and Career Decision-Making Difficulties in a French Sample: The Mediating Role of Career Decision Self-Efficacy

In this paper, published in Journal of Career Assessment, we examine links between ambiguity tolerance and career decision-making difficulties, highlighting the mediating role of career decision self-efficacy.

Are Reflective Models Appropriate for Very Short Scales? Proofs of Concept of Formative Models Using the Ten-Item Personality Inventory

In this paper, published in Journal of Personality, we examine whether reflective models are suitable for very short scales and present proofs of concept for formative modeling using the Ten-Item Personality Inventory.

A Snapshot of g? Binary and Polytomous Item-Response Theory Investigations of the Last Series of the Standard Progressive Matrices (SPM-LS)

In this paper, published in Intelligence, we propose the use of the last series of the last series of the Standard Progressive Matrices (SPM-LS) as a viable snapshot measure of g. We provide an extensive Item-Response Theory (IRT) analysis of the instrument, based on the binary (pass/fail) and nominal (response categories) data.

Measuring “Good Taste” with the Visual Aesthetic Sensitivity Test-Revised (VAST-R)

In this paper, published in Personality and Individual Differences, we examine psychometric properties of the VAST-R and discuss its use as a measure of visual aesthetic sensitivity (“good taste”).

A Cross-Cultural Study of Task Specificity in Creativity

In this paper, published in Journal of Creative Behavior, we investigate whether creativity scores are task-specific across cultural contexts, and discuss implications for how creativity should be measured and interpreted.

Is the Questionnaire of Cognitive and Affective Empathy Measuring Two or Five Dimensions? Evidence in a French Sample

In this paper, published in Psychiatry Research, we test whether the Questionnaire of Cognitive and Affective Empathy is better represented by a two- or five-dimension structure in a French sample.

How Does Creative Giftedness Differ from Academic Giftedness? A Multidimensional Model

In this paper, published in Learning and Individual Differences, we compare profiles of creative and academic giftedness using a multidimensional framework.

Psychometric Properties of the French Ten-Item Personality Inventory

In this paper, published in Journal of Individual Differences, we evaluate reliability and structure of the French Ten-Item Personality Inventory.

Individual Differences in Aesthetic Ability: The Case for an Aesthetic Quotient

In this paper, published in Frontiers in Psychology, we propose and evaluate an aesthetic quotient framework for individual differences in aesthetic ability.

Appraising the Duality of Self-Monitoring: Psychometric Qualities of the Revised Self-Monitoring Scale and the Concern for Appropriateness Scale in French

In this paper, published in Canadian Journal of Behavioural Science, we report French psychometric validation results for revised self-monitoring measures.

French Validation of the Overexcitability Questionnaire 2: Psychometric Properties in a High-Potential Sample

In this paper, published in Journal of Personality Assessment, we validate the Overexcitability Questionnaire II in a French high-potential sample.

Learning to Judge Creativity: The Underlying Mechanisms in Creativity Training for Non-Expert Judges

In this paper, published in Learning and Individual Differences, we investigate how non-expert judges can be trained to evaluate creativity and examine the mechanisms underlying gains in judgment quality.
