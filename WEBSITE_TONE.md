# Website Tone

Use this note when writing or revising short text that appears on the personal website.

## Overall Tone

- Professional, clear, and understated.
- Intelligent without sounding self-important.
- Warm enough to feel personal, but not chatty.
- Confident, but never promotional or inflated.

## Style Preferences

- Prefer plain, precise sentences over ornate language.
- Keep wording concise.
- Sound like an academic speaking clearly to a broad audience.
- Favor specificity over vague praise or abstract claims.
- Preserve the current site voice and visual identity; do not introduce flashy copy unless explicitly asked.

## Good Fit

- Short bios
- Section intros
- Publication/package blurbs
- Contact-page text
- Homepage copy

## Avoid

- Corporate or consulting language
- Generic AI phrasing
- Hype words like `cutting-edge`, `transformative`, `innovative` unless truly necessary
- Overly casual or jokey tone
- Long blocks of abstract academic prose

## For Academic-Sounding Website Text

- Be direct.
- Use concrete nouns and verbs.
- If a claim is important, state why in simple terms.
- Prefer measured confidence to grand claims.

## For Personal Website Pages

- Keep first-person voice natural.
- Do not oversell achievements.
- Let the facts do the work.
- When in doubt, choose restrained and clear over impressive-sounding.
